Homelab philosophy

A homelab is a place to experiment, learn, break things, and fix them (and repeat), experience the lows of what-went-wrong and the highs of having-fixed-it. My homelab is where I practice real-world skills in an enterprise-like environment: secure network design, monitoring, log analysis and automation. While at it I make sure to have fun, be creative and get a little poetry out of it. More practically speaking, I meddle with:

  • Server administration and hardening
  • Applied security principles - architecture and implementation
  • Network segmentation + controlled remote access
  • Virtualization and services (Proxmox, containers, monitoring)
  • Log-centric workflows: parsing, triage, detection thinking
  • Automation with Python/Bash (and selective AI assistance)
  • Self expression and imagination
